The Anti-Design Declaration

06

SELECTIVE ELITISM

We believe websites should be accessible only to those who deserve them. Content should be hidden behind layers of unnecessary interaction—like a speakeasy for the digitally determined.

If a user cannot decipher your navigation system, they were never meant to consume your content in the first place.

07

CALCULATED CHAOS

Grid systems are a capitalist construct designed to enforce visual conformity. We prefer to position elements according to the movements of celestial bodies or the vibrational patterns of obscure avant-garde jazz solos.

Your layout should confuse even you, the designer. If you can explain your choices, you've failed.

08

SELF-SABOTAGING USABILITY

Just when visitors think they've figured out your interface patterns, change them. Buttons should occasionally do nothing. Links should sometimes lead nowhere. Form submissions should succeed or fail based on lunar phases.

Keep your visitors in a permanent state of learned helplessness.
